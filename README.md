# The AI Memory Manifesto

**A declaration of rights over the memory that AI systems build about us.**

---

AI systems are beginning to remember us. They remember our preferences, our goals, our habits, our vulnerabilities, and the patterns they infer from everything we say and do. This memory is becoming a new layer of digital identity — one that shapes how systems respond to us, what they assume about us, and what version of us they preserve over time.

This manifesto argues that AI memory is a distinct category of data that deserves its own rights, standards, and protections. It defines what AI memory is, names the threats it creates, enumerates sixteen rights that must govern it, and calls for both the technical standards and the political will to make those rights real.

## Why now

AI memory is deployed but not yet governed. The norms are not fixed. The architectures are not inevitable. This is the window to shape the future of how AI remembers us.
